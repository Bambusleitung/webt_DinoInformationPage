Theories of their extinction

Probable theories

Meteor

The most well-known theory is the meteor theory.
In 1980 two scientists proposed the notion
a meteor the size of a mountain hit into Earth 66 million years ago.

The key piece of evidence is an oddly high amount of the metal iridium in
the geologic boundary zone that seems to cap any known rock layers containing dinosaur fossils.
The theory gained even more steam when scientists were able to link the extinction event
to a huge crater along the coast of Mexico’s Yucatán Peninsula.

Volcanic fury

Old lava flows in India known as the Deccan Traps also seem to match in time with the end of the Cretaceous,
with massive outpourings of lava spewing forth between 60 and 65 million years ago.

A vast eruption would've choked the sky with carbon dioxide
and other gases that would have dramatically changed Earth’s climate.
This event coud've killed most of the animals that lived then.
